Why EcoSort AI?

Waste segregation is the first and most critical step in the circular economy. Traditional recycling models fail due to high contamination rates—when incorrect materials end up in recycling streams, entire batches are sent to landfills.

EcoSort AI uses state-of-the-art Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to analyze waste items in milliseconds, guiding users with immediate, actionable segregation steps. This eliminates guesswork and reduces stream contamination by up to 85%.

By digitizing waste intelligence, we empower individuals, facilities managers, and municipalities to track sustainability performance dynamically and verify environmental impact with transparent, real-time metrics.

98% Prediction Accuracy

Trained on extensive datasets of consumer waste including complex plastic shapes, paper varieties, and multi-colored alloys.

Real-time Deep Learning

Utilizes optimized MobileNetV2 architecture running fast CPU-based inference to classify objects immediately upon upload or camera frame capture.

How the AI Model Works

The underlying technology that powers our circular economy classifier.

1

Image Capture

User uploads an image file or captures a live frame from the device camera.

2

Preprocessing

OpenCV color-converts BGR to RGB, resizes to 224x224, and normalizes pixel arrays.

3

CNN Inference

MobileNetV2 extracts deep features and scores 1000 standard ImageNet classes.

4

Score Aggregation

Top predictions map to categories (Plastic, Paper, Metal, Organic) using substring matching.

Material Impact & Guidelines

Why precise segregation makes an immediate difference to our planet.

Plastic

  • Saves raw petroleum resources
  • Mitigates microplastic water pollution
  • Recycled plastics power textiles & packaging

Paper & Cardboard

  • 1 ton of recycled paper saves 17 trees
  • Uses 40% less energy than virgin pulp
  • Must keep oil-free to avoid bale rejection

Metal & Alloys

  • Saves 95% energy compared to raw bauxite
  • Infinitely recyclable with zero quality loss
  • Reduces destructive open-pit mining

Organic Waste

  • Composting traps carbon in the soil
  • Prevents toxic landfill methane emissions
  • Creates nutrient-rich natural fertilizers
