Common Waste Architectures

Understanding the core materials that power our circular economy.

Plastic

Flexible, durable, and highly pervasive. It takes hundreds of years to degrade naturally, making aggressive AI-powered recycling strategies essential to prevent micro-pollution.

Paper & Cardboard

Made from crushed wood pulp and cellulose. Highly recyclable, but oil contamination renders it unusable. Segregation at the source ensures clean bales for paper mills.

Metal & Alloys

Aluminum and steel cans are infinitely recyclable without loss of quality. Reclaiming scrap metal saves up to 95% of the energy needed compared to raw ore mining.

Organic Waste

Biologically degradable materials like food scraps and yard trimmings. When composted, it sequesters carbon and replenishes soil; thrown in landfills, it creates toxic methane gas.
